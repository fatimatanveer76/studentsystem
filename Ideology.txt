Ideology of Pakistan | A set of beliefs, ideas, and principles guiding a nation’s political, social, cultural, and economic systems.  
Two-Nation Theory | Muslims and Hindus are two distinct nations with different religions, cultures, languages, and social systems.  
Role of Islam | Foundation of Pakistan’s ideology emphasizing equality, justice, unity, brotherhood, and moral values.  
Historical Background | Developed from Sir Syed Ahmed Khan’s reforms, Allama Iqbal’s Allahabad Address (1930), and Quaid-e-Azam’s leadership.  
Objectives Resolution (1949) | Declared sovereignty belongs to Allah; ensures democracy, freedom, equality, justice, and minority rights.  
Importance of Ideology | Provides national unity, stability, direction, purpose, and identity for future generations.  
Principle of Pakistan
Responsibilities of Citizens | Follow Islamic values, promote unity, obey laws, and work for national development.  
Conclusion | Ideology rooted in Islam and Two-Nation Theory, guiding Pakistan’s existence and progress.
